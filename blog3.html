What is the Risk-Reward Ratio—and Why Does it Matter?

The risk-reward ratio helps traders evaluate whether a trade is worth taking by comparing how much you could lose (risk) to how much you could gain (reward).

For example, a 1:3 ratio means you're risking ₹1 to potentially earn ₹3. This allows traders to:

  • Set realistic expectations
  • Compare multiple trade opportunities
  • Make consistent, logic-based decisions instead of emotional ones

Some traders flip the script and use a reward-risk ratio, which focuses on potential profit first (e.g., 3:1 instead of 1:3). The goal remains the same: seek trades where the reward outweighs the risk.

How to Calculate the Risk-Reward Ratio

To calculate the risk-reward ratio, you'll need three key pieces of information:

  • Entry Price – The price at which you enter the trade
  • Target Price – Your ideal profit-taking price
  • Stop-Loss Price – The exit point to minimize losses if the trade moves against you

Formula:

Risk = Entry Price – Stop-Loss
Reward = Target Price – Entry Price
Risk-Reward Ratio = Risk ÷ Reward

Example:

  • Entry Price = ₹165
  • Target Price = ₹180 → Reward = ₹15
  • Stop-Loss = ₹160 → Risk = ₹5

Risk-Reward Ratio = ₹5 ÷ ₹15 = 1:3

This means you're aiming to earn ₹3 for every ₹1 you risk—a solid setup for most traders.

Tips for Using the Risk-Reward Ratio Wisely

Here are some best practices to maximize the effectiveness of this strategy:

  1. Set Realistic Targets
    Base your price targets and stop-loss levels on historical data and current market trends—not on wishful thinking.
  2. Stick to Your Plan
    Always follow your stop-loss strategy. Avoid the temptation to “wait it out” when a trade goes south.
  3. Factor in Market Volatility
    Volatile assets may require wider stop-losses and more flexible reward targets. Adjust accordingly.
  4. Manage Your Capital
    Never risk more than you can afford to lose. Keep your trade sizes proportional to your overall portfolio.

Pros and Cons of the Risk-Reward Ratio

Benefits:

  • Promotes structured decision-making
  • Helps manage and limit financial risk
  • Makes comparing different trades quick and easy

Limitations:

  • Doesn’t account for the probability of success
  • Relies heavily on accurate target and stop-loss predictions
  • Can be disrupted by unexpected market movements

Conclusion: Risk-Reward is Your Trading Compass

Whether you're a complete beginner or starting to trade more seriously, the risk-reward ratio is a critical concept to understand. It’s not just about how much you can make—it’s about making sure you don’t lose more than you can handle.

By using this ratio consistently, you’ll be able to trade with greater confidence, discipline, and resilience—no matter the market conditions.
